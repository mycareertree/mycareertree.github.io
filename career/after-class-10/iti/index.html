ITI Courses After Class 10

Path: Class 10 → ITI (Industrial Training Institute)

ITI courses focus on hands-on skills and job readiness. They are designed for students who want to enter the workforce early with practical technical expertise.

What Is ITI?

ITI (Industrial Training Institute) courses are skill-based programs that train students in specific trades required by industries.

ITIs are run by government and private institutes under the Directorate General of Training (DGT).

Popular ITI Trades

  • Electrician
  • Fitter
  • Welder
  • Turner
  • Machinist
  • Mechanic (Diesel / Motor Vehicle)
  • COPA (Computer Operator & Programming Assistant)

Jobs After ITI

Many ITI students start working immediately after completion.

  • Technician & trade-specific jobs
  • Manufacturing plants & workshops
  • Construction & infrastructure projects
  • Service & maintenance roles

Apprenticeship & Skill Growth

Apprenticeship training helps ITI students gain real industry experience.

  • Apprenticeship under NAPS & NATS
  • Paid on-the-job training
  • Improves employability & skills

Government Jobs After ITI

ITI certificate holders are eligible for various government jobs.

  • Railways (Group C & D technical posts)
  • PSUs & state departments
  • Defence technical roles
Government Job Opportunities

Entrepreneurship & Self-Employment

Many ITI graduates start their own work.

  • Electrical, mechanical, or repair services
  • Small fabrication or workshop business
  • Contract-based technical services

Reality Check (Important)

ITI provides early entry into jobs, but growth depends on experience, skill upgrades, and continuous learning.

Salaries start modest but improve significantly with specialization and years of hands-on work.
